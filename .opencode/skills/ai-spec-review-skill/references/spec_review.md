# Specification Quality Review Heuristics

## Focus areas
- Completeness of functional and non-functional requirements
- Clarity and precision of language
- Internal consistency across sections
- Testability of each requirement
- Traceability from goals to requirements to acceptance criteria

## Common risks
- Requirements that use vague language ("appropriate", "fast", "secure") without measurable criteria
- Contradictory statements across sections
- Implicit requirements that are never stated
- Requirements that cannot be verified through testing
- Missing stakeholder scenarios or user roles
- Scope boundaries that are assumed but not documented

## Review questions
- Can every requirement be implemented without asking the author for clarification?
- Are all terms defined once and used consistently throughout?
- Does every requirement have at least one testable acceptance criterion?
- Are there contradictions between different sections of the specification?
- Which stakeholders or user roles are mentioned but not fully specified?
- What assumptions has the reader been forced to make?
